A Traveler's Handbook

Parlance of the Tavern

Commands ye shall need, listed as a new traveler would discover them:

look (l) — Survey the tavern and its occupants. A good first step upon entering any unfamiliar place.
examine [target] (x) — Take a closer look at someone or something. Use a number from the look list, a name, or a keyword.
talk to [patron] (t) — Strike up a conversation with a patron or the barkeep. You may use their name or a number from the look list.
end conversation — Walk away from whoever you are talking to. Always polite to say farewell first.
order [drink/food] — Order food or drink from the barkeep. Works anytime, no conversation needed.
menu (m) — See what food and drink the barkeep serves. Prices vary by establishment.
shop (s) — Browse the barkeep's selection of trinkets and curiosities for sale.
buy [item] (b) — Purchase a trinket from the barkeep's shop. Gold is deducted from your purse.
use [item] — Use an item from your inventory. Effects depend on the item.
give [item] — Give an item to whoever you are talking to. You must be in conversation. Example: give ale
give [amount] gold — Hand over some of your gold to a patron you are speaking with. Example: give 5 gold
challenge — Challenge the patron you are speaking with to a bar game for gold. If they accept, you choose which game to play from the ones they know.
take [item] — Pick up an interactable item from the tavern environment and add it to your inventory.
examine inv [item] — Inspect an item in your inventory for details, history, or lore.
status — Check your current state: name, condition, location, and active conversations.
help (?) — Show the in-game command reference sign posted on the tavern wall.
quit (q) — Leave the tavern and end the session. Your progress can be saved first.
While in conversation, prefix commands with / (e.g., /look, /order ale). Plain text is spoken directly to whoever you are talking to.

The Measure of a Man's Drink

As you drink, your condition changes through five stages:

0 — Clear-headed — No effects. Sharp as a blade and steady on your feet.
1 — Merry — Mild speech slurring begins. A pleasant warmth colors the room. The world sways very gently.
2 — In his cups — Noticeable slurring in your words. The text window blurs and the sway grows more pronounced.
3 — Three sheets to the wind — Heavy garbling of speech. The screen sways strongly and vision becomes quite blurred.
4 — Gone to the saints — Nearly incoherent. Maximum visual distortion. You may not last much longer on your feet.
Both effects are real: your typed speech garbles (others may struggle to understand you) and the screen itself blurs and sways. Food and water will sober you up.

The Art of Wagering

Challenge any patron to a game of chance for gold.

Use the challenge command while speaking with a patron to propose a wager. If they accept, they'll tell you which games they know — you choose which one to play. Both players stake gold before the game begins; the winner takes all. Not every patron will accept — some are too cautious, some too proud, and some have already lost their purse to another traveler tonight. Watch how they react to winning and losing — it reveals much about their character.

───────────────────

Coin and Commerce

Gold makes the tavern go round.

You begin each session with a purse of gold, shown in the side panel. Spend it wisely: food and drink can be ordered anytime (use menu to see prices). Water is always free and will help clear your head if the ale gets the better of you. Trinkets are purchased from the shop (shop to browse, buy to purchase). Gold can be earned by winning bar games or by receiving gifts from generous patrons. You may also give gold directly to a patron mid-conversation with give [amount] gold — some appreciate the gesture more than others.

───────────────────

Satchel and Sundries

Your inventory holds trinkets, gifts, and curiosities.

Items are acquired by buying from the barkeep's shop, receiving gifts from patrons, or picking up objects left around the tavern with take. Your current inventory is always visible in the side panel. Use use [item] to activate an item, give [item] to offer it to whoever you are speaking with, and examine inv [item] to read its description and lore. Items are often conversation starters — the right gift given to the right person can open unexpected doors.

───────────────────

Saving Your Tale

Your adventure persists between visits.

Type save at any time to save your progress: your gold, inventory, relationships, and the state of the tavern are all preserved. The next time you visit, click CONTINUE on the title screen to resume exactly where you left off — same tavern, same patrons, same unfinished business. Clicking NEW GAME will ask for confirmation before overwriting your existing save, so you will never lose progress by accident.

Words of Wisdom

Save before high-stakes wagers — fortune is fickle.
A wise traveler keeps coin in reserve. The barkeep does not extend credit.
Examine everything — the tavern holds secrets for the observant.
Each patron has their own story. Talk to everyone.
The barkeep's shop restocks with unique trinkets each session.
In conversation, speak naturally — patrons respond to what you say, not just commands.

Of Taverns and Their Keepers

A note on the world you've entered.

Every tavern in these lands is its own world. Each has a different name, a different barkeep with their own history and temperament, and a rotating cast of patrons who have wandered in from roads you have never walked. No two sessions are alike — what happens tonight is shaped entirely by your choices, your words, and the luck of who happens to be drinking when you push open the door.

The patrons are not scripted. Each carries a personality, a background, secrets they may or may not share, and opinions that shift based on how you treat them. Be curious. Be bold. Be generous, or be ruthless — the tavern will remember.
